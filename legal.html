Section 04 · Accessibility Statement

Accessibility Statement

We want everyone to be able to use this site and attend our nights. We work to meet WCAG 2.1 Level AA standards across the website and to make our events welcoming and accessible.

On the Website

  • Semantic HTML, descriptive page titles, and proper heading hierarchy so screen readers can navigate.
  • Sufficient color contrast on body text and primary actions.
  • Keyboard navigation supported throughout. Tab order is logical and focus is visible.
  • Alt text on meaningful images. Decorative images are marked as such so screen readers can skip them.
  • The interactive bingo game on the Play page honors prefers-reduced-motion. Pulse and tilt animations are disabled if your system has reduced motion turned on.
  • A voice "yell to win" feature is included as a fun extra; a button alternative is always available for players who cannot or do not want to use voice input.

At Events

  • Venues are selected with step-free access where possible. Specific venue accessibility is listed on the Events page for each night. If it's not listed, ask us before you buy.
  • Hosts speak through a PA and song titles also appear on a visible screen so hearing-impaired players can follow along.
  • We can pre-print large-format bingo cards if you let us know at least 48 hours before the event.
  • Service animals are always welcome.

Known Limitations

  • The Web Speech API used by the in-game "yell to win" feature is not supported on all browsers (notably older Safari). The on-screen "I yelled it" button is the fallback.
  • Some user-submitted gallery images may be missing detailed alt text. We are working to add this.

Feedback

If you hit an accessibility barrier on this site or at one of our nights, please tell us, we want to fix it. Email info@takemebackbingo.com with the page (or event) and what went wrong, and we'll respond within 5 business days.
